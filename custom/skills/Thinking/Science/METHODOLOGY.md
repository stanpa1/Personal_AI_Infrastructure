# Scientific Methodology

## The Cycle

1. **Observe** — Gather facts. What is actually happening? (Not what you think is happening.)
2. **Question** — What specifically are we trying to understand?
3. **Hypothesize** — Propose explanations. Each must be falsifiable.
4. **Predict** — If hypothesis is true, what should we observe?
5. **Test** — Design experiments to confirm or refute predictions.
6. **Analyze** — What do the results tell us?
7. **Conclude** — Which hypothesis survived testing? What's the next question?

## Key Principles

- **Falsifiability** — A hypothesis must be testable and refutable
- **Control variables** — Change one thing at a time
- **Reproducibility** — Can the result be repeated?
- **Occam's Razor** — Prefer the simplest explanation that fits the data
- **Null hypothesis** — Consider "nothing unusual is happening" as a valid explanation
- **Bias awareness** — Watch for confirmation bias, recency bias, survivorship bias

## Common Pitfalls

- Jumping to conclusions before testing
- Testing the easy hypothesis instead of the likely one
- Ignoring evidence that contradicts your theory
- Stopping investigation after first plausible explanation
- Confusing correlation with causation
